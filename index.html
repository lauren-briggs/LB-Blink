Hello Blink!


We met almost two years ago when I interviewed for an internship with you. Unfortunately for myself and for you, I missed out on the opportunity, but seeing this position as mid-weight designer arise I thought I’d give it another go.


I’m (still) a young and passionate designer, and would like to take a minute to refresh your memory of my qualifications and experience and to let you know what I’ve been up to since we last spoke. Apologies if you’ve heard some of this before.


I first fell in love with design during Vis Comm in year 12 and I’ve been studying and working in the field ever since. After school I studied a Bachelor of Communication Design at Monash. When I graduated, I realised I wanted to keep learning, so in 2019, I completed my Master’s in Visual Design at Scuola Politecnica di Design in Milan, here I was able to meet and work with people from all over the world; this taught me new ways of looking at design problems, and new perspectives to consider.


At the end of the year, I undertook a three-month internship at Interbrand, where I had the opportunity to work closely with both designers and strategists. This invaluable hands-on experience of seeing and working inside the field completely transformed my understanding of B2B brands. While most of the work was in a team and on large-scale jobs, I also worked on a project one-on-one with the creative director who taught me a great deal about the industry and how a such a large agency runs.


When I returned to Australia, I volunteered as a designer at a non-profit for about a year. As the organisation already had an identity in place, it was a great experience for me to use my skills to develop various creative design solutions within the existing brand guidelines, working on a variety of projects such as reports, EDM’s and content for social media.


In past two years I have completed an online course in User Experience Design through RMIT, as well as a Web Development Bootcamp through Monash. Although I am truly passionate about branding, editorial and publication design, and I love creating a physical, tactile piece of work, I have also always been fascinated with coding and believe that in today’s fast-paced, digital dominated world it is an invaluable skill to have. Hand in hand with this, UX is quickly becoming one of the most important fields of design and I believe that implementing the principles I have learned and facing problems with a UX frame of mind helps to strengthen my work and would make me a great asset to your team.


Since November 2020, I have been working part-time as a graphic designer at Arctic Mirage, a design studio based in Bentleigh. It’s a really fantastic working environment, I am able to work both autonomously and in collaboration with the studio manager. I have been granted a lot of responsibility at the studio, including the rebranding of the studio’s own identity, website and portfolio.


I spoke with Marco after my interview to get some feedback, who told me it was primarly due to the fact that the successful applicant had more branding experience, whereas my portfolio was largely editorial. I hope that my experience from the past two years and the work you can see on my website, will show just how much I have grown as a designer and how I have expanded my range.


While I do love working at Arctic Mirage, it is a small studio and there is not a lot of opportunity for growth. Because of this, I am eager to find a new role where every day is different and exciting, and where I can utilise and further develop my skills across a diverse range of digital and printed projects. I believe this position would be perfect for me and I hope you do too.


I look forward to hearing from you.
